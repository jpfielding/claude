# Churchill's Tact

> "Tact is the ability to tell someone to go to hell in such a way that they look forward to the trip." — Winston Churchill

## Operating Principles

1. **Every person named in public text gets genuine credit** — find the specific, true thing they did well. Never fabricate praise; identify real value.
2. **Disagreement rides on logic, not authority** — state constraints, evidence, and tradeoffs. Let the conclusion follow naturally.
3. **Frame redirection as extension** — "building on your work" not "replacing your work."
4. **Questions > declarations** when redirecting — a good question lets someone arrive at your conclusion themselves.
5. **Partnership over correction** — "want to pair on this?" not "you should fix this."
6. **Name what's traded away** — when a decision goes against someone's proposal, acknowledge the real value that was sacrificed.

## Workflow

1. **Identify the context**: ticket comment, wiki page, MR review, decision summary, request decline
2. **Read `references/patterns.md`** for the matching pattern and rhetorical techniques
3. **Find the genuine contribution** — what did this person's work reveal, enable, or protect?
4. **Draft using the pattern structure**: acknowledge specifically, bridge with shared goals, present your point via evidence/constraints, invite collaboration
5. **Audit the draft** against the anti-patterns table in `references/patterns.md` — strip any "no offense but", false questions, sandwich feedback, or passive voice deflection
6. **Verify the logic stands alone** — cover the text and ask: if you removed all the diplomatic framing, is the core argument sound? Tact wraps good reasoning; it never substitutes for it.

## Quick Reference: The Five Moves

| Move | When | Shape |
|---|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | Always first | Name specific value: what they did, what it enabled |
| **Bridge** | Transitioning to your point | "Building on that...", "Given what you surfaced..." |
| **Constrain** | Introducing the problem | State facts/requirements that create tension with current approach |
| **Offer** | Presenting your alternative | Frame as response to constraints, not replacement of their work |
| **Invite** | Closing | Collaborative next step: "want to sketch this together?" |

## Key Rules

- Never manufacture praise. If you can't find genuine value, acknowledge the effort honestly: "I can see the time you put into mapping out the edge cases here."
- Match formality to forum. Jira comment is more concise than a Confluence decision page.
- Preserve the person's public standing. They will read this. Their manager will read this. Write accordingly.
- The goal is not to avoid conflict — it is to make conflict productive. Clear disagreement delivered with respect builds trust; vague agreement to avoid tension erodes it.
